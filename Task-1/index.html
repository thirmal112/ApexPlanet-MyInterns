Prime Minister of India

Head of Government, Republic of India

Incumbent: Narendra Modi (since 26 May 2014)

The Prime Minister of India is the head of government and the chief executive of the Republic of India. The Prime Minister leads the Union Council of Ministers and is responsible for running the government and implementing policies.

The office was established on 15 August 1947, with Jawaharlal Nehru as the first Prime Minister. Since then, India has had several Prime Ministers, each contributing to the nation’s development and governance.

The Prime Minister is appointed by the President of India and must have the confidence of the Lok Sabha, the lower house of Parliament. The Prime Minister’s official residence is 7, Lok Kalyan Marg, New Delhi.

Narendra Modi is the current Prime Minister, serving since 2014, and is known for his leadership, reforms, and international diplomacy.
